// about

Teaching by doing.

Led primarily by undergraduate students, CompNeuroSociety is Florida State University's computational neuroscience club - we run workshops, journal clubs, guest speaker events, and collaborative projects to build skills and explore careers in this interdisciplinary field.

// our mission

To foster a community that supplements the education of those curious about computational neuroscience through interactive workshops, engaging journal clubs, and collaborative projects. By providing these opportunities, we aim to increase the confidence of our members in the field, preparing them for future endeavors while giving space to discuss interests in the field.

// what is computational neuroscience?

The study of the brain's function using mathematical models, computer simulations, and other computational techniques. It's a multidisciplinary field that combines neuroscience with computer science, mathematics, and other disciplines.

// faq
Do I need coding or neuroscience experience to join?

No. CompNeuroSociety at Florida State University is open to any FSU student curious about computational neuroscience, regardless of major or experience level.

Is CompNeuroSociety open to every major?

Yes. Membership is open to all currently enrolled FSU students - you don't need to be a neuroscience or computer science major to join.

Does CompNeuroSociety have a hazing or discrimination policy?

Yes. We hold a zero-hazing policy and do not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, national origin, gender, sexual orientation, age, disability, or veteran status.

Do I have to attend every meeting?

No. Active participation is encouraged but flexible - involvement naturally rises and falls with coursework, jobs, and life, as long as you stay in touch with the board.
